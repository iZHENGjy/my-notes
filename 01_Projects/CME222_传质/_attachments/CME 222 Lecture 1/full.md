# What is Mass Transfer??

• Mass transfer is defined as the transports of one or more chemical species in fluid mixture due to some driving force   
• Driving force: concentration differences, temperature differences, pressure differences, difference due to gravity, magnetic or electrical field

# Diffusion

• Diffusion is the transport of molecules, ions, or small particles from a region of relatively high concentration into a region of lower concentration   
• In the absence of a concentration gradient, molecules continue to move randomly (Brownian motion), but no net mass transfer occurs and the system remains uniform

# Diffusion

![](images/9ebd1415a4af2cf2b8e30cb1ffbbd98498d3bc0da04228b0e264f0fea45bad16.jpg)

Mass diffusion is in the direction of decreasing concentration   
After a sufficient time, uniform concentrations of A and B are achieved   
There is no net transport of species A or B across the imaginary plane

# Convection

• Mass transfer between a moving fluid and a surface or between immiscible moving fluids   
• Affected by the dynamics characteristic of the flowing fluid   
• Fluid motion combines with diffusion to promote the transfer of a species for which there exists a concentration gradient   
• Analogous to convection heat transfer
