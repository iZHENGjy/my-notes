# Dimension

$$
D _ {A B} = \frac {- J _ {A , z}}{d c _ {A} / d z} = \left(\frac {N}{L ^ {2} t}\right) \left(\frac {1}{N / L ^ {3} \cdot 1 / L}\right) = \frac {L ^ {2}}{t}

Unit: cm2/s or m2/s

• Depends on the pressure, temperature, composition of system, medium of diffusion   
Diffusion coefficients/mass diffusivity

For gases: 10-6 to 10-5 m2/s

For liquids: 10-10 to 10-9 m2/s,

For solids: 10-14 to 10-10 m2/s

# Equality of Diffusion Coefficient

Only true for a binary system of constant molar density (concentration)   
• Occur at constant temperature and pressure for gas system   
One mole of A move in z direction, one mole of B move in opposite direction

$$
\begin{array}{l} c _ {A} + c _ {B} = c = \frac {P}{R T} \\ J _ {A} = - J _ {B} \\ - D _ {A B} \frac {d c _ {A}}{d z} = - (- D _ {B A} \frac {d c _ {B}}{d z}) \\ D _ {A B} = D _ {B A} \\ \end{array}
$$

# Mean Free Path

• A molecule travels in a straight line before collision   
• The distance travelled by a molecule before collision is termed free path   
• Mean free path: average distance travelled by a molecule between two successive collision   
• Mean free path increases as the number of molecules per unit volume decreases (less collision)

# Lennard-Jones Diameter/Collision Diameter

When two gas molecules approach one another, they cannot come closer beyond a certain distance   
• Is the closest distance between the centers of the two molecules taking part in a collision   
• Is the distance at which the intermolecular potential between the two particles is zero   
Related to the mean free path: smaller the collision or molecular diameter, the larger the mean free path
